About Us

Alex Bessonov

Alex is a Senior Engineering Leader and Executive with over 20 years of experience in the Software and Security Industry. Prior to founding BitClave, a stealth-mode startup, Alex has served as the Founder and General Manager at whiteCryption, a security and cryptography-oriented company. Alex led whiteCryption to millions of dollars of revenue, while at the same time amassing numerous Fortune 500 clients. More recently, Alex has served as the Chief Security Officer at LG Electronics, overseeing all product lines as well as heading a large effort to remodel and centralize the company’s security team. In the past, Alex has also held multiple leadership and engineering positions at companies such as NXP, Microsoft, and eBay. His corporate experience aside, Alex’s true passion is building teams and products. Alex currently holds advisory roles in ventures such as Alchemist Accelerator, .406 Ventures, and HMG Strategy, LLC. Feel free to reach out to Alex if you want to pitch him an idea, talk about your product or just introduce yourself. Contact Alex at his email address.

Patrick Tague

Patrick is a researcher working in the area of security and privacy for mobile and wireless systems with primary focus on the Internet of Things and additional interests in statistical modeling and data analytics. Prior to joining BitClave, Patrick held a variety of research, teaching, and administrative responsibilities as an Associate Research Professor in the ECE Department and the Associate Director of the Information Networking Institute at Carnegie Mellon University, based on the CMU Silicon Valley campus, where he founded and directed the Mobile, Embedded, and Wireless Security research group. Patrick earned his PhD in Electrical Engineering from the University of Washington and did his undergraduate work in Computer Engineering and Math at the University of Minnesota. Contact Patrick at his email address.

Emmanuel Owusu

Emmanuel Owusu is the Chief Architect at BitClave and currently a Ph.D. candidate in the Engineering and Public Policy Department at Carnegie Mellon University. Emmanuel’s professional experience includes research and development at Adobe, Intel Labs, Bosch Research, and IBM Research. Additionally, Emmanuel is a US Patent Holder and has been honored as an NSF Graduate Research Fellow and a Cisco Internet of Things Security Grand Challenge winner. As the Head of Technology at EEme, Emmanuel lead the technical product development effort for EEme's cloud scale energy analytics solutions. Contact Emmanuel at his email address.
